# Elicitation Methods

## 5-Phase Elicitation Structure
1. Goal Clarification — What and why
2. User Identification — Who and how
3. Problem Validation — Is this real
4. Scope Boundary — In vs out
5. Success Criteria — How we measure

## Core Reflective Methods
- **Expand or Contract**: Broaden or narrow the scope of discussion
- **Explain Reasoning**: Walk through logic and assumptions
- **Critique and Refine**: Challenge assumptions, identify weaknesses

## Structural Analysis Methods
- **Logical Flow Analysis**: Check consistency and completeness of reasoning
- **Goal Alignment Assessment**: Verify alignment with stated objectives

## Risk and Challenge Methods
- **Identify Potential Risks**: What could go wrong? Probability × impact
- **Critical Perspective**: Devil's advocate on proposed approach

## Creative Exploration Methods
- **Tree of Thoughts**: Explore multiple solution paths simultaneously
- **Hindsight Reflection**: If this project failed, what would we wish we'd considered?

## Multi-Persona Methods
- **Agile Team Perspective**: Consider from developer, tester, user, PM viewpoints
- **Stakeholder Roundtable**: Consider from different user/business stakeholder angles
- **Meta-Prompting**: Step back and evaluate the elicitation process itself

## Advanced Techniques
- **Self-Consistency Validation**: Generate multiple independent answers, check agreement
- **Red Team vs Blue Team**: Argue for and against the proposed approach
- **Innovation Tournament**: Compare competing solution ideas head-to-head

## Technique Selection by Context
- **Technical Content**: Tree of Thoughts, Meta-Prompting
- **User-Facing Content**: Stakeholder Roundtable, Agile Team
- **Strategic Content**: Red Team vs Blue Team, Hindsight Reflection
- **Risk Assessment**: Identify Risks, Critical Perspective
